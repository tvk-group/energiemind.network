Mining Heat Utility

Convert compute waste heat into usable thermal energy

Mining Heat Utility programs capture excess heat from compute infrastructure and route it to on-site or district heating systems. Hotels, greenhouses and agricultural facilities benefit from low-cost thermal input while hosting efficient compute loads.

Heat Capture

Liquid-cooled or air-cooled recovery systems integrated with existing HVAC.

Thermal Routing

Buffer tanks, heat exchangers and distribution to greenhouses, pools or building loops.

Load Balancing

Compute scheduling aligned with thermal demand curves for maximum utility.

Monitoring

Real-time dashboards for temperature, flow rate and energy savings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Partner network and pilot program details

Who can apply as a pilot partner?

Hotels, farms, solar sites, greenhouse operators, mining hosts, commercial building owners, data center operators and energy infrastructure partners with suitable electrical or thermal capacity may apply.

What does the pilot program include?

Pilot partners receive site assessment, integration planning, deployment support, monitoring dashboards and access to the EnergieMIND partner network for knowledge sharing and scale planning.

Is there a cost to join the network?

Application is free. Pilot terms vary by site type and integration scope. Each partnership is structured around shared energy savings, compute revenue and operational outcomes.

How does mining heat utility work?

Compute hardware generates heat during operation. Recovery systems capture this thermal energy and route it to building heating, greenhouse climate control, pool systems or industrial processes.

Can I join if I only have solar generation?

Yes. Solar site owners can integrate flexible compute loads to improve self-consumption and optionally add heat recovery if a thermal offtaker is available nearby.

What is ENM's role in the network?

ENM is envisioned as a future ecosystem utility for network coordination and partner incentives. The current focus is on pilot deployments and infrastructure partnerships, not token sales.

Which regions are supported?

The EnergieMIND Network accepts applications globally. Regional pilot coordinators assist with local compliance, grid connection and utility integration requirements.

How long does the application process take?

Initial review typically occurs within two weeks. Site assessment and pilot planning timelines depend on project scope and local permitting requirements.
